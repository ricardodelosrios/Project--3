Candlestick Patterns

Candlestick patterns are powerful tools for technical analysis in stock trading. They provide valuable insights into potential price reversals, continuations, and trend strength. Below, you'll find descriptions of the top 5 bullish, bearish, and continuation candlestick patterns.

Top 5 Bullish Candlestick Patterns

  1. Bullish Engulfing Pattern:

    The Bullish Engulfing Pattern is a powerful two-candlestick pattern that signals a reversal from a downtrend to an uptrend. It occurs when a small bearish candle is followed by a larger bullish candle that completely engulfs the previous candle's range. This pattern suggests that buying pressure has overwhelmed selling pressure, indicating potential bullish momentum.

  2. Hammer:

    The Hammer is a single-candlestick pattern characterized by a small body and a long lower shadow. It forms after a price decline and signals a potential bullish reversal. The long lower shadow indicates that buyers have pushed the price up significantly from its lows, and it's often seen as a sign of strength.

  3. Doji:



    The Doji is a single-candlestick pattern that represents market indecision. It occurs when the opening and closing prices are very close or the same, resulting in a small or non-existent body. A Doji can signal a potential reversal or continuation, depending on the preceding price action.

  4. Piercing Pattern:

    The Piercing Pattern is a two-candlestick bullish reversal pattern. It starts with a bearish candle, followed by a bullish candle that opens below the previous day's close and closes above the midpoint of the first candle's body. This pattern suggests a potential shift from a downtrend to an uptrend.

  5. Morning Star:

    The Morning Star is a three-candlestick bullish reversal pattern. It begins with a long bearish candle, followed by a small candle (can be bullish or bearish) that gaps down, and finally, a long bullish candle that closes above the midpoint of the first candle. This pattern indicates a potential trend reversal.

Top 5 Bearish Candlestick Patterns

  1. Bearish Engulfing Pattern:

    The Bearish Engulfing Pattern is a two-candlestick pattern that signals a reversal from an uptrend to a downtrend. It occurs when a small bullish candle is followed by a larger bearish candle that completely engulfs the previous candle's range. This pattern implies that selling pressure has surpassed buying pressure, indicating potential bearish momentum.

  2. Shooting Star:

    The Shooting Star is a single-candlestick pattern characterized by a small body and a long upper shadow. It forms after an uptrend and suggests a potential bearish reversal. The long upper shadow indicates that sellers pushed the price down significantly from its highs, signaling possible weakness.

  3. Evening Star:

    The Evening Star is a three-candlestick bearish reversal pattern. It begins with a long bullish candle, followed by a small candle (can be bullish or bearish) that gaps up, and finally, a long bearish candle that closes below the midpoint of the first candle. This pattern indicates a potential shift from an uptrend to a downtrend.

  4. Dark Cloud Cover:

    The Dark Cloud Cover is a two-candlestick bearish reversal pattern. It starts with a bullish candle, followed by a bearish candle that opens above the previous day's close and closes below the midpoint of the first candle. This pattern suggests a potential reversal of the current trend.

  5. Bearish Harami:

    The Bearish Harami is a two-candlestick bearish reversal pattern. It occurs when a large bullish candle is followed by a smaller bearish candle completely contained within the previous candle's body. This pattern indicates a potential trend reversal.
